1. INTRODUCTION
Access to quality fitness guidance remains a significant challenge in many communities due to financial constraints, privacy concerns, and social barriers. A large population is unable to afford personal trainers or feels uncomfortable exercising in public gym environments, leading them to rely on unguided home workouts that often result in inconsistency and incorrect exercise form.
Existing digital fitness applications attempt to address this issue but fall short in critical areas. Most platforms provide static workout plans or basic tracking features without real-time feedback or true personalization, while also neglecting user privacy concerns.
BloomFit is an AI-powered, privacy-first fitness ecosystem designed to democratize access to intelligent workout guidance. It combines a Flutter-based mobile application with a React-based Web Companion to deliver real-time feedback, dynamic workout generation, and personalized fitness experiences without requiring physical trainers or gym access.
The system uses Google Gemini 3 Flash to generate adaptive workout pathways based on user goals, experience level, equipment availability, and historical performance. To ensure correct exercise execution, BloomFit integrates a real-time biomechanical analysis system using MediaPipe, which tracks 3D skeletal landmarks to perform rep counting, posture correction, and accuracy evaluation.
A key feature of BloomFit is its privacy-first design. All motion capture and video processing are performed locally on the user’s device, with only minimal data such as repetition counts and feedback signals transmitted through Firebase.
Additionally, the system incorporates a continuous feedback loop where user inputs and performance metrics are analyzed to refine future workouts, enabling the platform to function as an adaptive digital trainer that evolves with the user.
This project aims to provide an accessible, intelligent, and private fitness solution that empowers individuals to maintain consistent and safe workout routines from their homes.
1.1 MOTIVATION (PROBLEM STATEMENT)
Access to effective fitness guidance remains a significant challenge due to multiple real-world barriers, particularly in regions where affordability, privacy, and accessibility are major concerns. While digital fitness applications have grown rapidly, they fail to address several fundamental issues that impact user consistency, safety, and long-term adherence.
The key challenges motivating this project are as follows:
● Financial and Accessibility Barriers: Professional fitness guidance through personal trainers or gym memberships is often expensive and inaccessible to a large population. As a result, many individuals rely on unguided home workouts, which lack structured supervision and reduce effectiveness.
● Privacy and Social Discomfort: A considerable number of users avoid gym environments due to social anxiety, fear of judgment, or cultural constraints. This creates a strong preference for home-based workouts, where users feel more comfortable but lack proper guidance and feedback.
● Lack of Personalization and Adaptivity: Existing fitness applications primarily rely on static or template-based workout programs. These systems fail to adapt to individual user needs, physical conditions, injuries, or performance variations, leading to ineffective and monotonous workout experiences.
● Absence of Real-Time Feedback and Accuracy Measurement: Users performing exercises at home have no reliable mechanism to verify whether their posture and movements are correct. The lack of real-time feedback leads to incorrect form, reduced workout efficiency, and increased risk of injury.
● Low Engagement and Consistency: Fitness requires long-term commitment, but many applications fail to maintain user motivation. The absence of adaptive feedback loops, meaningful progress tracking, and intelligent recommendations results in poor adherence and high dropout rates.
BloomFit is motivated by the need to address these interconnected challenges through a unified and intelligent system. By integrating AI-driven dynamic workout generation, real-time biomechanical analysis, and a privacy-first architecture, the platform aims to provide accessible, personalized, and safe fitness guidance.
This project aligns with the EPICS philosophy by focusing on solving a community-relevant problem and delivering a scalable solution that improves health accessibility, promotes consistency, and reduces barriers to fitness for a wider population.

2. EXISTING WORK / LITERATURE REVIEW
The development of Bloomfit is grounded in an extensive analysis of the current digital health ecosystem, which intersects exercise science, human-computer interaction (HCI), and advancements in artificial intelligence and computer vision. With the rapid growth of mobile fitness applications, there has been a significant shift toward digital solutions for health and wellness. However, despite this growth, existing systems continue to exhibit critical limitations in terms of personalization, real-time feedback, user engagement, and privacy. This literature review explores these limitations in detail and identifies the research gaps that motivate the proposed system.
2.1 REVIEW OF EXISTING FITNESS APPLICATIONS AND MARKET GAPS
The digital fitness landscape is broadly categorized into multiple types of applications, each addressing specific aspects of fitness but failing to provide a comprehensive solution. These categories include streaming-based platforms, template-driven workout planners, and passive tracking applications. While these systems have contributed to making fitness more accessible, they suffer from inherent limitations that lead to high user churn rates and low long-term adherence.
Research in mobile health systems highlights that lack of personalization and adaptability is one of the primary reasons for user disengagement. Studies indicate that users are more likely to abandon fitness applications when the content does not evolve based on their performance or changing physical conditions (Zhou et al., 2018; Monteiro-Guerra et al., 2019). This highlights the need for systems that can dynamically adapt to individual users rather than relying on static models.
2.1.1 STREAMING AND TEMPLATE-BASED SERVICES
Streaming-based fitness platforms and template-driven workout applications are among the most widely used solutions in the digital fitness domain. These platforms provide structured workout routines, often designed by professional trainers, and deliver them through video-based or pre-defined formats.
While these systems offer convenience and accessibility, they fundamentally lack personalization. Workouts are designed for a general audience and do not account for individual differences such as fitness level, injury history, or real-time performance. As a result, users often experience either undertraining or overtraining, both of which negatively impact fitness outcomes.
Furthermore, these systems operate on static programming models, where the workout plan remains fixed regardless of user feedback. Research in adaptive systems suggests that such static approaches are unable to respond effectively to dynamic human conditions, such as fatigue accumulation or unexpected physical discomfort (Zhou et al., 2018). This rigidity limits the system’s ability to provide safe and effective training over time.
Another major limitation is the absence of feedback mechanisms. Users are expected to follow along with video instructions without any form of validation or correction. This creates a disconnect between instruction and execution, increasing the likelihood of incorrect form and potential injury.
2.1.2 PASSIVE TRACKING AND LOGGING APPLICATIONS
Another major category of fitness applications includes passive tracking systems that focus on recording workout-related data such as sets, repetitions, duration, and calories burned. These applications are widely used by experienced users who already possess knowledge of structured training programs.
While these tools are effective for data aggregation and performance tracking, they lack proactive intelligence and guidance. The responsibility of designing workouts, adjusting intensity, and ensuring progression lies entirely with the user. For beginners or individuals without formal training knowledge, this can be a significant barrier.
Research indicates that passive systems are insufficient for promoting long-term engagement, as users require guidance, feedback, and motivation to maintain consistency (Monteiro-Guerra et al., 2019). Additionally, these systems do not incorporate contextual information such as user fatigue, injury, or recovery status, which are essential for effective training.
The primary gap across both categories is the absence of a system that combines structured engagement with intelligent, context-aware adaptability.
2.2 GAMIFICATION AND BEHAVIORAL SCIENCE IN HABIT FORMATION
In addition to technical limitations, user engagement plays a critical role in the success of fitness applications. The effectiveness of a fitness system is not solely determined by the quality of workouts but also by the user’s ability to adhere to the program over time.
Research in behavioral science and HCI emphasizes the importance of motivation, reinforcement, and habit formation in sustaining long-term engagement. Gamification has emerged as a popular strategy to address these challenges by incorporating game-like elements such as rewards, streaks, levels, and progress tracking.
Gamified systems provide immediate psychological rewards, which help bridge the gap between effort and outcome. This is particularly important in fitness, where physical results are often delayed. By providing instant feedback and visible progress, gamification enhances user motivation and encourages consistent participation.
2.2.1 THE DUOLINGO MODEL OF PROGRESSION
One of the most successful implementations of gamification is the pathway-based progression model popularized by learning platforms. In this model, user activities are structured into sequential units and lessons, creating a clear and visually engaging roadmap.
Key elements of this approach include:
Visual progression pathways that reduce cognitive load and provide clear goals
Streak systems that leverage behavioral principles such as loss aversion
Experience points (XP) and leveling systems that provide immediate feedback
These mechanisms have been shown to significantly improve user retention and consistency. However, research also indicates that while gamification enhances engagement, it does not address deeper issues such as personalization, adaptability, and exercise accuracy (Monteiro-Guerra et al., 2019). Without intelligent feedback and dynamic adjustments, gamification alone is insufficient to ensure effective training outcomes.
2.3 ADAPTIVE PROGRAMMING THROUGH LARGE LANGUAGE MODELS (LLMS)
Recent advancements in artificial intelligence have introduced new possibilities for adaptive fitness systems. Machine learning and AI-driven approaches enable the development of systems that can analyze user data and generate personalized recommendations in real time.
Large Language Models (LLMs) represent a significant advancement in this domain. These models are capable of understanding complex user inputs and generating context-aware responses. In the context of fitness applications, LLMs can be used to generate dynamic workout plans, modify exercises based on user feedback, and provide personalized coaching.
Research demonstrates that AI-based personalization significantly improves user engagement and reduces dropout rates by tailoring content to individual preferences and performance (Li, 2024). Unlike traditional rule-based systems, AI-driven systems can adapt non-linearly, allowing for continuous improvement and refinement of workout plans.
2.3.1 CONTEXTUAL AND GENERATIVE CAPABILITIES
One of the key advantages of AI systems is their ability to generate content dynamically based on context. These systems can incorporate multiple variables such as user goals, experience level, available equipment, and historical performance to create customized workout plans.
Additionally, AI systems can process natural language inputs, allowing users to request modifications in real time. For example, users can adjust workout intensity, duration, or exercise selection based on their current condition. This level of flexibility is not possible in traditional systems.
2.3.2 THE ADAPTIVE FEEDBACK LOOP (RPE)
A critical component of adaptive systems is the integration of feedback mechanisms. The Rate of Perceived Exertion (RPE) is widely used in exercise science as a subjective measure of workout intensity. Research indicates that RPE is a reliable indicator for adjusting training load and preventing overtraining.
By incorporating RPE and pain feedback into the system, AI models can dynamically adjust workout intensity and structure. For instance, high RPE values may trigger a reduction in volume or intensity, while low RPE values may lead to progressive overload. This creates a continuous feedback loop that enhances both safety and effectiveness (Monteiro-Guerra et al., 2019).
2.4 COMPUTER VISION IN FITNESS APPLICATIONS
Computer vision technologies have played a crucial role in enabling real-time analysis of human movement. Pose estimation techniques allow systems to detect skeletal landmarks and track body movements with high precision.
Frameworks such as MediaPipe provide real-time detection of three-dimensional landmarks, enabling biomechanical analysis through joint-angle calculations. These systems form the foundation for applications that require motion tracking, posture correction, and activity recognition.
Recent studies demonstrate that pose estimation frameworks can be effectively used for exercise posture recognition and repetition counting (Supanich et al., 2023; Ram et al., 2024). These systems provide immediate feedback, allowing users to correct their form during workouts.
Further research highlights that MediaPipe-based pose estimation achieves reliable accuracy in physiotherapy and rehabilitation settings, making it suitable for real-world fitness applications (Simoes et al., 2024). This is particularly important for home-based workouts, where users do not have access to professional supervision.
In addition, several systems have been developed for posture correction using deep learning and skeletal tracking techniques. These systems analyze joint angles and movement patterns to identify incorrect posture and provide corrective feedback (Agarwal et al., 2021; Raza et al., 2023; Chen and Yang, 2020).
2.5 PRIVACY AND ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS
While AI and computer vision technologies offer significant advantages, they also introduce concerns related to privacy and data security. Many existing systems rely on cloud-based video processing, where user data is transmitted and stored on remote servers.
This raises concerns regarding unauthorized access, data misuse, and user discomfort, particularly in home environments. Studies in digital health emphasize the importance of privacy-preserving systems that minimize data exposure and ensure user control over personal information.
Approaches that perform computation locally on the user’s device, often referred to as edge computing, are gaining importance in addressing these concerns. By processing data locally and transmitting only minimal metadata, such systems can provide advanced functionality while maintaining user privacy.
2.6 IDENTIFIED RESEARCH GAPS
Based on the analysis of existing systems and research literature, the following gaps are identified:
● Lack of integration between dynamic AI-based workout generation and real-time feedback
● Limited availability of accurate biomechanical analysis in consumer fitness applications
● Insufficient focus on privacy-preserving fitness systems
● Absence of continuous feedback loops for adaptive personalization
Bloomfit addresses these gaps by combining AI-driven personalization, real-time biomechanical analysis, and a privacy-first architecture into a unified and scalable system.

3.2 WORKING PRINCIPLE
The working principle of BloomFit is based on the coordinated interaction between the mobile application, cloud backend, AI modules, and the motion capture system. The system operates as a continuous pipeline that integrates user input, real-time processing, and adaptive feedback to deliver a personalized fitness experience. Each component in the system contributes to a closed-loop workflow where data is continuously collected, processed, and utilized to refine future interactions and recommendations.
The process begins with user authentication and onboarding, where the user logs in through Firebase Authentication. Upon successful authentication, a secure session is established and the user profile is retrieved from Cloud Firestore. This profile contains essential information such as fitness goals, workout history, preferences, and previous performance data. This data acts as the foundation for personalization, enabling the system to generate workouts that are aligned with the user’s capabilities and objectives.
Once the profile data is available, the system activates the AI-based workout generation module, which creates a structured workout plan consisting of exercises, repetitions, sets, and progression logic. The generated plan follows standard fitness principles such as balanced muscle targeting, recovery considerations, and gradual progression. The system ensures that the workout remains adaptable by incorporating user-specific constraints such as available equipment, time limitations, and physical conditions.
The frontend of the application follows a structured and unidirectional data flow model, as illustrated in Figure 2. User interactions, such as selecting a workout or modifying preferences, trigger state updates through a centralized logic layer. This layer communicates with data repositories to fetch or update information from Firestore. The retrieved data is processed and stored in an immutable state, which ensures consistency and predictability in application behavior.
This architecture ensures that any change in data automatically triggers a UI update, allowing the interface to remain synchronized with backend operations. It also minimizes inconsistencies and improves performance by maintaining a clear separation between data handling and presentation logic. As a result, the user experiences a smooth and responsive interface, even during real-time updates.
Figure 2. Frontend Data Flow (Riverpod)
This diagram represents the unidirectional data flow within the mobile application. User actions trigger a Riverpod-based state management system, which interacts with data repositories to fetch information from Firestore. The updated data is stored in an immutable state, causing UI components to rebuild and reflect the latest application state.
When a workout session is initiated, the system establishes a connection between the mobile application and the web companion using a pairing code mechanism. This pairing process creates a unique live session document in Firestore, which acts as a shared communication channel between the two components. Through this session, both the mobile application and the motion capture module remain synchronized in real time, enabling continuous data exchange during the workout.
The user performs exercises in front of the camera, and the motion capture module processes the video feed using MediaPipe pose estimation. The system detects key skeletal landmarks corresponding to major joints such as shoulders, elbows, hips, and knees. Using these landmarks, joint angles and relative positions are computed to track body movement and posture throughout the exercise.
Based on this processed data, the system performs real-time movement analysis. Each exercise is associated with predefined movement patterns and threshold values, which define the correct range of motion. The system continuously compares the user’s movement against these thresholds to determine whether a repetition is valid. In addition to repetition counting, the system also identifies deviations in posture, such as incorrect joint alignment or incomplete motion.
The accuracy evaluation mechanism ensures that only correctly performed repetitions are counted, improving the reliability of performance tracking. When an incorrect form is detected, the system generates immediate feedback in the form of correction cues or indicators. This feedback is transmitted to the mobile application as lightweight metadata, such as repetition count updates and error flags, ensuring low latency and efficient communication without transmitting raw video data.
The mobile application reflects this feedback in real time, allowing the user to adjust their posture and improve execution during the workout itself. This immediate feedback loop enhances both safety and effectiveness, reducing the risk of injury and promoting correct exercise habits.
After completing the workout, the system collects performance-related inputs such as Rate of Perceived Exertion (RPE), skipped exercises, and any discomfort experienced by the user. These inputs provide subjective insights into the user’s experience and are stored in Firestore along with objective performance data.
The adaptive feedback mechanism is illustrated in Figure 3, which represents the AI-driven feedback loop responsible for modifying future workouts. The collected data is analyzed by the AI module to evaluate whether the workout intensity and structure were appropriate. Based on this analysis, the system dynamically adjusts future workout plans by either reducing intensity, modifying exercises, or applying progressive overload.
This combination of real-time evaluation and post-workout adaptation enables BloomFit to function as an intelligent and responsive system that continuously evolves based on user performance and feedback.
Figure 3. AI Feedback Loop Logic
This diagram illustrates how post-workout feedback, including RPE and pain data, is processed by the AI module. Based on the analysis, the system determines whether the workout intensity was appropriate. If the user experiences high exertion, the system reduces intensity or generates an easier variation. If the workout is completed comfortably, progressive overload is applied to enhance performance. The updated workout plan is then stored and reflected in the user’s pathway.
This feedback loop enables the system to continuously adapt and improve workout recommendations, ensuring both safety and effectiveness. By combining objective performance data, such as repetition accuracy and completion rate, with subjective inputs like RPE and discomfort levels, the system develops a more comprehensive understanding of the user’s physical condition. The updated workout pathway is then synchronized with the mobile application through Firestore, ensuring that all modifications are reflected instantly in the user interface. This completes the cycle of input, processing, and adaptation, forming a closed-loop system that evolves over time.
The adaptive nature of this system allows it to respond dynamically to user performance. For instance, consistent high exertion or repeated errors in form may result in reduced intensity or alternative exercise selection, while stable performance and low exertion may trigger progressive overload to enhance fitness gains. This ensures that workouts remain both challenging and safe, tailored to the user’s evolving capabilities.
Through the integration of structured data flow, real-time motion analysis, and AI-driven feedback, BloomFit functions as an intelligent and evolving fitness system. The system continuously learns from user behavior, performance trends, and feedback patterns to refine its recommendations. Over time, this results in increasingly personalized workout experiences that optimize effectiveness, improve adherence, and promote long-term fitness consistency.
3.3 AI-BASED WORKOUT GENERATION
The AI-based workout generation module is a core component of the BloomFit system, responsible for creating personalized, adaptive, and context-aware workout pathways. Unlike traditional fitness applications that rely on static templates or predefined routines, BloomFit utilizes a generative AI approach to dynamically construct workout plans based on individual user data.
The process begins with the collection of user-specific inputs during onboarding and subsequent usage. These inputs include fitness goals, experience level, available equipment, workout preferences, and historical performance data. This information is structured and passed to the AI engine through the Path Generation Service, which acts as an intermediary between the mobile application and the backend AI module.
The AI engine processes these inputs to generate a multi-day workout pathway, consisting of structured sessions that include exercises, sets, repetitions, and rest intervals. The generated plan follows established fitness principles such as balanced muscle group targeting, recovery cycles, and progressive overload. This ensures that the workout is not only personalized but also scientifically structured.
A key feature of this module is its ability to perform dynamic adaptation. Unlike rule-based systems that follow fixed logic, the generative model can modify workout plans based on real-time feedback and historical trends. For example, if a user consistently reports high exertion or skips certain exercises, the system adjusts future sessions by reducing intensity, modifying exercises, or redistributing workload.
The system also supports context-aware modifications, allowing users to request changes in real time. Through the conversational AI interface, users can modify workouts based on immediate constraints such as time availability, fatigue, or equipment limitations. The AI interprets these inputs and generates updated workout structures accordingly.

3.4 REAL-TIME ACCURACY AND POSE ESTIMATION
The real-time accuracy and pose estimation module is a critical component of the BloomFit system, responsible for evaluating exercise performance and ensuring correct movement execution. This module enables the system to provide immediate feedback to users, reducing dependency on human trainers and improving workout safety and effectiveness.
The system utilizes MediaPipe pose estimation to detect human body landmarks from the camera feed. MediaPipe identifies a set of key skeletal points corresponding to major joints such as the shoulders, elbows, hips, knees, and ankles. These landmarks are tracked in real time, forming the basis for motion analysis.
Using the detected landmarks, the system performs joint angle calculations to evaluate posture and movement. Angles between connected joints are computed using vector-based methods, allowing the system to determine the range of motion during each exercise. For example, in exercises such as squats or arm curls, specific joint angles define the correct execution of a movement.
Each exercise is associated with predefined movement thresholds, which define the acceptable range of motion for a valid repetition. These thresholds are derived based on standard exercise biomechanics and ensure that only properly executed movements are counted. The system continuously compares the user’s movement against these thresholds to detect transitions between different phases of an exercise.
The system implements a state-based repetition detection mechanism, where each exercise is divided into stages such as starting position, mid-movement, and completion. A repetition is counted only when the user successfully transitions through all required stages while maintaining correct form. This approach minimizes false positives and improves the accuracy of repetition counting.
In addition to repetition detection, the system performs real-time accuracy evaluation by analyzing deviations in posture. If the user performs an exercise with incorrect alignment or insufficient range of motion, the system identifies the error and generates corrective feedback. This feedback is communicated to the mobile application in real time, allowing the user to adjust their posture immediately.
To ensure efficiency, all motion analysis is performed locally on the user’s device within the web companion. This eliminates the need to transmit video data to the cloud, significantly reducing latency and preserving user privacy. Only processed metadata, such as repetition counts and feedback signals, is transmitted to the backend.
The system is designed to operate under real-time constraints, ensuring that feedback is delivered with minimal delay. This is essential for maintaining a smooth user experience and enabling immediate correction during exercise execution.
Overall, the integration of pose estimation, joint angle analysis, and state-based repetition detection enables BloomFit to provide accurate, real-time feedback. This transforms the system into an intelligent fitness assistant capable of guiding users through exercises with precision and reliability.
